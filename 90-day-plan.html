Operating premise

Earn trust through the work.

A 90-day plan for a technical consulting manager entering an established interdisciplinary practice.

00

Entry posture

This plan is a hypothesis for discovery. It does not presume undisclosed EY methods, team structure or client programs. Its purpose is to show how Russell would learn quickly, contribute credibly and make physical-AI delivery easier to operate.

First principle

Do not arrive with a replacement methodology. Read the system EY already has: technical standards, alliance capabilities, engagement mechanics, review authorities, reusable assets and the informal practices that make delivery work.

Success at day 90

  • Trusted by one delivery team and client workstream.
  • Clear boundary between Russell's credible technical contribution and specialist depth.
  • One useful workstream outcome or artifact delivered.
  • One evidence continuity improvement adopted without process burden.
  • One grounded opportunity for reusable capability or additional service.

Days 0-30

Read the system

Learn the technical, client and commercial operating environment before prescribing.

30

Technical immersion

  • Map the actual robotics, simulation, data, MLOps, DevOps and cloud toolchain used by the team.
  • Review representative architecture, test, deployment, safety and handoff artifacts.
  • Pair with deeper specialists in ROS, Omniverse/Isaac, controls, perception and ML workflows.
  • Build a precise learning backlog tied to active work rather than generic certification.

Engagement immersion

  • Shadow daily client sessions and internal delivery rituals.
  • Understand the engagement economics, scope boundaries and expansion logic.
  • Map decision rights across EY, alliance partners and the client.
  • Identify recurring seams where technical evidence becomes hard for clients to act on.

Risks to avoid

Over-indexing on management language; creating parallel process; claiming stack depth not yet demonstrated; translating client complexity into an oversimplified dashboard; waiting too long to produce useful work.

Days 31-60

Lead a bounded seam

Contribute directly while making the workstream easier to understand and operate.

60

Own defined work

  • Lead a bounded integration, test, evidence, operating-readiness or client-handoff seam.
  • Maintain commitments, dependencies, decisions and acceptance evidence.
  • Use specialist review explicitly; do not substitute generalist confidence for technical proof.
  • Translate technical findings into client consequences without stripping nuance.

Introduce only useful mechanism

  • Prototype the Reality Transfer Case beside existing artifacts.
  • Test whether it reduces ambiguity across mission, environment, perception, authority, run state and learning.
  • Remove fields that do not change decisions.
  • Keep essential engineering evidence in its native systems.

  • One coaching or knowledge-sharing session that improves team decision quality.

Days 61-90

Transfer and compound

Close the loop with the client, the team and the practice.

90

Transfer operating ownership

  • Confirm client or support ownership, authority, observability, incident and improvement responsibilities.
  • Make unresolved assumptions and residual risks explicit.
  • Verify that the handoff is usable in the client's actual cadence.
  • Capture what should trigger rollback, retraining, re-simulation or human-first operation.

Harvest practice leverage

  • Separate reusable engineering or delivery patterns from client-specific content.
  • Quantify delivery effort and rework categories where evidence exists.
  • Identify alliance, accelerators or capability gaps that matter repeatedly.
  • Frame additional services only where the current work proves a real need.
