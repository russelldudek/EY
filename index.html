Operating artifact

A physical-AI workstream should carry one coherent case from virtual development into live work. The case does not replace ROS, Omniverse, MLflow, SIL/HIL or engineering test evidence. It makes that evidence legible to the people who must approve, operate, fund and sustain the system.

Hard objection

The honest gap is real.

Russell should not be presented as a career ROS/Omniverse robotics engineer.

The posting asks for recent, deep hands-on ownership across ROS, C++/Java, NVIDIA Omniverse, digital twins, reinforcement learning, MLflow, cloud ML pipelines and SIL/HIL. The verified evidence does not support claiming that exact stack or tenure.

The defensible hiring question is narrower and more useful: can Russell help EY bind physical-AI specialists into a client-trusted delivery system while staying close enough to the technology to challenge assumptions, connect evidence, manage the workstream, develop people, protect economics and transfer capability?

Not claimedProduction ownership of ROS, Omniverse, MLflow, HIL or named hyperscale ML platforms.
Testable valuePhysical-systems judgment, technical-operational translation, high-reliability controls, client-facing mechanisms and delivery leadership.
First 90 days

Earn trust through the work

The entry plan assumes discovery, not undisclosed knowledge of EY methods or client programs. It is designed to establish technical credibility, make delivery evidence coherent and create reusable assets without slowing the team.

Days 0-30

Read the system

  • Map EY physical-AI capabilities, alliance assets, engineering standards and review authorities.
  • Shadow active client workstreams and technical rituals.
  • Identify one bounded contribution where Russell can produce useful evidence immediately.
  • Establish the engagement decision map: client outcome, owner, economics, technical dependencies and risk.
Days 31-60

Lead a bounded seam

  • Own a defined workstream or integration seam with clear acceptance evidence.
  • Install a lightweight Reality Transfer Case alongside existing engineering artifacts.
  • Clarify human authority, fallback, observability and handoff responsibilities.
  • Coach the team on decision records, operating consequences and client communication.
Days 61-90

Transfer and compound

  • Close one workstream with client-readable evidence and sustainment ownership.
  • Harvest reusable patterns without abstracting away client reality.
  • Review delivery economics, rework and additional-service hypotheses.
  • Propose the next capability or engagement based on proved need, not novelty.
Executive interview questions

Questions that reveal the real job

The strongest questions should uncover where EY currently loses confidence, continuity or leverage between technical proof and client operation.

Where does a promising physical-AI workstream most often lose momentum: simulation fidelity, real-world testing, client authority, production ownership or engagement economics?
How do EY teams divide decision rights among robotics engineering, industry experts, risk leaders, alliance partners and the client?
What evidence must exist before EY recommends moving from simulation to controlled physical testing?
Which physical-AI assets are meant to be reusable across engagements, and which must remain client-specific?
How is manager success balanced across technical contribution, team leadership, client trust, utilization, margin and business development?
What would make you say after six months, “this person made the practice easier to scale without making it less rigorous”?
