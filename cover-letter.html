EY's Physical AI Engineering Manager role is compelling because it is not merely a robotics job. It asks someone to help convert simulation, sensing, machine learning and software delivery into client-ready capability - while leading a technical workstream, developing people, protecting engagement economics and building trust with the operators who will inherit the system.

That is the operating seam I have worked across throughout my career. I bring digital-twin experience related to warehouse automation, robotic pick/pack/stow, conveyance and material-flow decision support, together with current leadership in a software-enabled robotics and machine-vision environment. At Vape-Jet, I connect production, engineering, quality, support, telemetry and enterprise workflows around a fielded fleet of approximately 1,000 robots. The work is not abstract transformation; it is making machine and workflow evidence visible enough to prioritize, escalate, correct and sustain.
